About me:

Hey there, I'm Angie! I'm a software developer with a background in the digital arts, the kitchen, and the great outdoors. Before I dropped everything to study software full-time, I worked as a baker during the winter season and a river guide during the summer.

As a latchkey kid raised on Nintendo 64 and Saturday morning cartoons, I have been enamored of digital media for as long as I can remember. I was lucky enough to pursue this passion in college, earning my B.F.A. in Art Technology from the University of Oklahoma in 2018. While in school I specialized in interactive video installations and 3D animation, also receiving training in DSLR photography, video production, graphic design, and web development. After graduating I knew I wanted to pursue my passion for the outdoors, moving to Colorado and beginning work as a guide for a local five-star resort. I spent four excellent years developing my guest service and risk management skills as a professional guide, additionally picking up commercial kitchen work during the off-season. Let me tell you, there are few things in this world that force a body to consider time, space, and cost efficiency like commercial kitchen work. Despite how much I enjoyed the seasonal work, I found I sorely missed working with software, eventually enrolling in a full-stack web development bootcamp in order to pursue my passion for emerging technologies full-time. I pride myself on bringing the same efficiency, creative problem solving, and friendly attitude to my software work as I do to my other creative and professional pursuits.

When I'm not coding you can usually find me hiking through Colorado's beautiful 14rs, experimenting with my sourdough starter, or working on silly animations.
